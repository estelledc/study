A standard approach is to use a layered control architecture [101] where there is a hierarchical decomposition of actions from continuous time feedback controllers and reflexes all the way to longer-term and higher-level goals and intents. Promising steps in these directions include internal multimodal chain-of-thought approaches [64], which demonstrate how models can simulate future world states across modalities to support reasoning and action planning. We would like to propose, in addition to these exciting upcoming developments, the need for special attentional and internal feedback (from higher latent layers to input layers) mechanisms to represent, monitor, predict, and adjust relevant internal state variables. This would likely require modifications to the architecture (including recurrence) as well as to the reinforcement learning algorithms we use to incorporate state values, actions, and rewards, as well as additional data from simulated or actual interactions. This has been explored successfully for games with clear, verifiable rewards by systems such as AlphaZero and MuZero [102] where reinforcement learning from self-play experience proved superior to human data-based learning. The agents produced in this manner displayed superior transfer learning capability in novel game domains. Scaling these ends to produce better embodied benchmarks (with complex internal states and actions) in real-world robotic domains is still an open problem requiring further research. These advances may also yield not only more flexible and adaptive AIs, but also computational analogs for key cognitive functions long-theorized but difficult to isolate in biological systems.

For cognitive scientists, these developments offer key testbeds to examine long-standing theories of embodied cognition [37]. For example, predictive processing models can be evaluated by probing how embodied MLLMs resolve mismatches between expected and actual feedback. Comparator models of motor control can be instantiated through simulated proprioceptive error correction. Multi-agent MLLMs may even offer ways to test the emergence of joint attention or perspective-taking. These frameworks can ultimately help clarify how internal and external dynamics are integrated to support adaptive and agentic behaviors.

## 6 结论

Developing safer, human-aligned world models requires modeling internal states alongside external embodiment. Models capable of simulating and understanding embodiment in its multiple aspects can better navigate their environments and may reduce the risk of misaligned or maladaptive behaviors, including those that appear sociopathic or harmfully indifferent to others [63], and increase cooperative behaviors [103]. This article draws from principles of internal and external embodiment in humans as a potential means of resolving embodied limitations in MLLMs (see outstanding questions) to promote trust, greater alignment, and safety between AI systems and human users.

### 人类对齐的内部基准

Most MLLM benchmarks focus on external tasks like object manipulation or spatial reasoning. To advance toward human-aligned and self-regulating AI, we propose several benchmarks that evaluate internal embodiment.

#### 1. 模拟稳态任务

MLLMs could be tested in environments with internal state variables (e.g., energy, temperature) as well as stressors (e.g., pain) that fluctuate over time. Agents must maintain stability by choosing actions like "resting" or seeking "food," even when they conflict with external task goals. Performance would reflect the ability to prioritize internal stability and trade-offs.

#### 2. 亲社会基准

MLLMs could be placed in multi-agent environments where one's internal state affects the other's. Helping behaviors or resource sharing in response to distress would reflect prosocial inferences driven by internal modeling [105]. Empathy benchmarks could ultimately test whether MLLMs simulate what another agent might be "feeling."

#### 3. 自我监控与意识

Two questions arise around internal modeling of awareness. One is the ability of MLLM to consistently simulate specific personas, roles, and styles when instructed. For instance, prompts such as "Write my biography as if you were a Victorian poet," or more recently "Draw an image of me in the Ghibli style," allow the MLLM to consistently respond and generate text or images in the requested style. These role-playing cases are extremely interesting aspects of MLLMs, though such steering or style transfer can arise without any internal state estimation. It was commonly known that when asked to identify itself, DeepSeek (and other MLLMs) would commonly reply "ChatGPT," choosing the most probable token sequence in the context [106]. The second question concerns self-critique. This is an extremely useful capability in MLLMs that has been exploited in using argumentation as a way to arrive at correct answers for a given prompt. Yet critiquing a previous response does not require internal state estimates or models since the input is the observable output of a previous prompt/context. There is some evidence that LLMs exhibit a self-preference bias [107, 108] in judging their output as better than other model outputs, possibly because of stylistic similarity or a bias for reduced perplexity in responses [108], but fail to transfer to many self-recognition tasks [109]. Recent work such as SelfIE (Self-Interpretation of Embeddings) [110] uses a framework that enables LLMs to interpret their own embeddings in natural language by passing the embedding as context and asking specific queries, offering a possible approach to self-monitoring.

### 术语表

**主动推断（Active Inference）**：扎根于自由能原理的预测处理（Predictive Processing）的延伸。主动推断提出，智能体不仅更新内部信念以最小化预测误差，还采取具身行动主动改变环境以更好地匹配这些预测。

**身体图式（Body Schema）**：简化表征，例如缺乏对关节肌肉精确身体动力学（扭矩、力、应力）的表征。它对应于对我们四肢位置和运动的隐性知识，以及分离自我与他人的流体边界感。

**具身推理问答（Embodied Reasoning Question Answering, ERQA）**：基准，专门关注通过与物理世界的交互来评估具身能力。

**前向模型（Forward Model）**：无论是我们自身还是他人的行动，前向模型对于平滑、正确的运动/行动都至关重要，因为它提供运动的预测，该预测与传入的感官和本体感觉反馈进行比较，以生成引导校正的错误信号。

**超网络（Hypernetworks）**：为另一神经网络（称为目标网络）生成权重的神经网络。它们作为元学习器，产生可根据不同任务或输入动态调整的权重。它们为更快速训练以及持续学习和适应提供了潜力。

**推理时计算（Inference-time Compute）**：在 AI 语境中常被称为"思考"，指模型在训练后为响应新输入而生成输出所执行的计算。

**指令微调（Instruction Tuning）**：通过在使用自然语言指令与任务特定响应配对的数据集上进行微调来改善大语言模型的方法。

**内感受（Interoception）**：神经系统感知和整合身体内部信号的过程，它积极塑造对身体内部状态的意识。早期产生且先于反思，它是具身化和意识的基础。

**Moravec 悖论（Moravec's Paradox）**：AI 经常难以执行人类认为容易的任务，同时在人类认为复杂的任务上表现出色。文中示例展示了当肢体远离身体时，从静态帧中识别点光展示对人类来说清晰且简单。

**多模态思维链（Multimodal Chain of Thought）**：AI 中跨模态（如视觉、语言、触觉）整合信息的逐步推理。

**多模态大语言模型（Multimodal Large Language Models, MLLMs）**：也称大多模态模型（Large Multimodal Models），是大语言模型的多模态和下一代，在广泛的多模态刺激（如计算机视觉、视频和图像描述与生成）上展现出令人印象深刻的能力。由于其广泛性，多模态大语言模型也被称为大型世界模型（large world models）或多模态基础模型（multimodal foundation models）。

**无光学字符识别推理（Optical Character Recognition-free Reasoning）**：多模态大语言模型无需使用独立的光学字符识别步骤即可理解和推理图像中嵌入的文本内容的能力。相反，该模型联合处理视觉和文本元素。

**点光展示（Point-light Display）**：通常在人体关键关节处显示约十几个光点。人类甚至新生儿能够从稀疏的视觉线索中轻松且立即推断出人体结构。

**预测处理（Predictive Processing）**：认知神经科学中的理论框架，将大脑定位为分层预测机器。在此观点中，大脑持续生成关于传入感官输入的**自顶向下（top-down）**预测，这些预测随后与实际输入进行比较。大脑的最终目标是最小化这种差异，即预测误差。

**循环（Recurrence）**：网络架构，其中过去输出被反馈回模型，从而能够随时间维持内部状态。

**基于人类反馈的强化学习（Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback）**：基于人类偏好信号微调语言模型的训练技术。人类标注者比较模型响应，这些偏好用于训练奖励模型。然后使用强化学习更新语言模型以生成更符合人类的输出。

**自注意力（Self-attention）**：大语言模型中的关键机制，允许模型根据序列中不同词元的相对关系对其进行加权。每个词元（如一个词）与所有其他词元进行比较以计算注意力分数。这使模型能够决定输入的哪些部分具有最高的注意力分数，因此在上下文中对推导意义最相关。

**软机器人学（Soft Robotics）**：机器人学子领域，旨在从受生物系统和原理启发的柔性、可变形材料中创建人工智能。
